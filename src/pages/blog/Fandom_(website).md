<h1 id="the-evolution-of-fandom-from-wikia-to-a-cultural-phenomenon">The Evolution of Fandom: From Wikia to a Cultural Phenomenon</h1>
<h2 id="introduction">Introduction</h2>
<p>In the ever-evolving landscape of online communities and knowledge sharing, Fandom has emerged as a significant player. This article traces the journey of Fandom, formerly known as Wikia, from its inception to its current status as a cultural phenomenon. We&#39;ll explore its acquisitions, controversies, and the technology behind its success.</p>
<h3 id="the-early-days">The Early Days</h3>
<p>Fandom&#39;s roots can be traced back to Wikia, which was rebranded as &quot;Fandom powered by Wikia&quot; in October 2016. This move aimed to align the platform more closely with the Fandom website, emphasizing its focus on passionate communities.</p>
<h3 id="new-leadership-and-branding">New Leadership and Branding</h3>
<p>In December 2016, Fandom appointed Dorth Raphaely, a former general manager of Bleacher Report, as its chief content officer. This marked a shift toward a more media-oriented approach.</p>
<p>On May 18, 2017, Fandom underwent a significant rebranding, unveiling a refreshed logo, all-uppercase lettering, and a flat design. This makeover aimed to modernize its image and appeal to a broader audience.</p>
<h3 id="a-change-in-ownership">A Change in Ownership</h3>
<p>February 2018 brought a change in ownership, with former AOL CEO Jon Miller leading the charge, backed by private equity firm TPG Capital. Jon Miller took on the role of co-chairman alongside Jimmy Wales, while Andrew Doyle became the interim CEO. These changes set the stage for Fandom&#39;s next chapter.</p>
<h3 id="expansion-through-acquisitions">Expansion Through Acquisitions</h3>
<p>Fandom&#39;s growth continued with strategic acquisitions. In July 2018, it acquired Screen Junkies from Defy Media, expanding its portfolio in the entertainment sector. Later that year, in December, Fandom made a significant move by acquiring Curse LLC, gaining assets like Gamepedia and websites such as D&amp;D Beyond, Futhead, Muthead, and StrawPoll.me.</p>
<h3 id="leadership-evolution">Leadership Evolution</h3>
<p>The year 2019 saw another change in leadership, as former StubHub CEO Perkins Miller took over as CEO. This transition coincided with Wikia&#39;s full domain name change to fandom.com. This move reflected Fandom&#39;s commitment to its new brand identity.</p>
<h3 id="platform-evolution">Platform Evolution</h3>
<p>Fandom&#39;s dedication to improving its platform became evident in June 2019 when it embarked on rewriting its core platform. This effort aimed to update its software to a newer version, enhancing user experience and functionality. The release of the Unified Community Platform (UCP) in March 2020 marked a significant milestone in this journey.</p>
<h3 id="strategic-shifts">Strategic Shifts</h3>
<p>In 2020, Fandom made strategic decisions, including the sale of Curse Network properties to Magic Find, which included various gaming communities and news websites. Additionally, Fandom began migrating Gamepedia wikis to a fandom.com domain in November 2020 as part of its search engine optimization strategy.</p>
<h3 id="expanding-horizons">Expanding Horizons</h3>
<p>February 2021 brought more expansion as Fandom acquired Focus Multimedia, the retailer behind Fanatical, a platform specializing in digital games, ebooks, and related products. Furthermore, Fandom demonstrated its commitment to inclusivity by updating its terms of use policy to prohibit deadnaming transgender individuals across its websites in late March 2021.</p>
<h3 id="a-new-look">A New Look</h3>
<p>In June 2021, Fandom rolled out FandomDesktop, a redesigned theme for desktop devices. Two months later, in August, Fandom introduced a new visual identity, featuring new colors, a new logo, and the tagline &quot;For the love of fans.&quot;</p>
<h3 id="full-domain-migration">Full Domain Migration</h3>
<p>By late November/early December 2021, all remaining wikis under the wikia.org domain had migrated to the fandom.com domain, streamlining the brand&#39;s online presence.</p>
<h3 id="recent-developments">Recent Developments</h3>
<p>April 13, 2022, brought news of Hasbro&#39;s acquisition of D&amp;D Beyond from Fandom. However, Fandom also faced challenges, such as the shutdown of StrawPoll.me in August 2022.</p>
<h3 id="a-major-acquisition">A Major Acquisition</h3>
<p>On October 3, 2022, Fandom made headlines once again by acquiring GameSpot, Metacritic, TV Guide, GameFAQs, Giant Bomb, Cord Cutters News, and Comic Vine from Red Ventures. This acquisition further solidified Fandom&#39;s position in the media and entertainment industry.</p>
<h2 id="services-and-features">Services and Features</h2>
<h3 id="fandom-s-present">Fandom&#39;s Present</h3>
<p>Fandom communities serve as online encyclopedias specializing in various subjects. While they cover a wide range of topics, the most common focus is on popular fiction franchises, including films, TV shows, games, books, and other media. This aligns with Fandom&#39;s shift from Wikia to a platform emphasizing passionate fan communities.</p>
<p>Articles in Fandom communities offer comprehensive and detailed information on specific topics, often surpassing what can be found on Wikipedia. This depth is particularly valuable for niche interests and specific vocabulary.</p>
<h3 id="content-and-policies">Content and Policies</h3>
<p>Fandom communities adopt a more lenient approach to image policies compared to Wikimedia Foundation projects, allowing for more illustrations. However, all user-generated content must adhere to free licensing terms. Hate speech, libel, pornography, and copyright infringement are prohibited, while unique and original content is encouraged.</p>
<h3 id="collaborative-approach">Collaborative Approach</h3>
<p>Fandom communities operate on a consensus-based model, where decisions are made collectively. The founder&#39;s opinion carries no more weight than any other community member&#39;s opinion, fostering cooperation and inclusivity.</p>
<h2 id="technology">Technology</h2>
<h3 id="behind-the-scenes">Behind the Scenes</h3>
<p>As of October 2022, Fandom relies on a heavily modified version of MediaWiki software, emphasizing social features such as blogs, chat, badges, forums, and multimedia. These extensions enhance the user experience and promote engagement within the communities.</p>
<h3 id="open-source-initiatives">Open Source Initiatives</h3>
<p>Fandom has a history of contributing to open-source projects, with some innovations initially created by ArmchairGM. These innovations, referred to as &quot;social tools,&quot; are available under open-source licenses.</p>
<h2 id="entertainment-news">Entertainment News</h2>
<h3 id="the-birth-of-fandom">The Birth of Fandom</h3>
<p>In 2016, Fandom launched as an online entertainment media website. It introduced a new approach to content creation, utilizing volunteer contributors known as &quot;Fandom Contributors&quot; alongside an editorial team. Fandom focused on pop culture and fan-related topics, including video games, movies, and television shows. The platform featured fan opinions, interviews with property creators, reviews, and how-to guides, creating a space where fans could celebrate their passions.</p>
<h3 id="the-rebranding">The Rebranding</h3>
<p>In the same year, Fandom made a pivotal decision to rebrand the entire Wikia platform as Fandom, emphasizing its commitment to the fan community. This transition aimed to increase adoption of Fandom&#39;s wiki-specific applications on various app ecosystems.</p>
<h2 id="wiki-partnerships">Wiki Partnerships</h2>
<h3 id="official-collaborations">Official Collaborations</h3>
<p>Fandom forged official partnerships to create wikis vetted as the &quot;official&quot; encyclopedia or wiki for various properties. These partnerships involved collaborations with entities like Sony Online Entertainment, Roddenberry Enterprises, 2K</p>
<p> Games, Warner Bros Interactive, Lionsgate Media, and others. These collaborations resulted in dedicated wiki communities, featuring exclusive content and support for specific properties.</p>
<h2 id="esports">Esports</h2>
<h3 id="embracing-esports">Embracing Esports</h3>
<p>In 2021, Fandom entered the world of esports by managing and promoting esports tournaments and streams on Twitch for the United States Navy. This move highlighted Fandom&#39;s versatility in catering to a wide range of interests.</p>
<h2 id="fandom-games-youtube-channel-">Fandom Games (YouTube Channel)</h2>
<h3 id="the-transition">The Transition</h3>
<p>Following Fandom&#39;s acquisition of Curse, the Curse Entertainment YouTube channel was rebranded as Fandom Games. Under this banner, Fandom Games became known for producing &quot;Honest Game Trailers,&quot; a popular series that provides humorous and candid insights into the world of gaming.</p>
<h2 id="past-services">Past Services</h2>
<h3 id="openserving">OpenServing</h3>
<p>OpenServing was a short-lived web publishing project owned by Fandom. It offered free wiki hosting, allowing founders to retain revenue from site advertising. While it used a modified version of Wikimedia Foundation&#39;s MediaWiki software, it ultimately faded into obscurity in 2008.</p>
<h3 id="armchair-gm">Armchair GM</h3>
<p>Armchair GM was a sports forum and wiki site initially launched in 2006. Fandom acquired it in late 2006, incorporating its architecture into other Fandom sites. However, support for ArmchairGM&#39;s custom software ceased in 2010, leading to its discontinuation.</p>
<h2 id="search-engines">Search Engines</h2>
<h3 id="exploring-search">Exploring Search</h3>
<p>Fandom&#39;s early foray into the world of search engines included the proposal of a copyleft search engine named &quot;Wikiasari&quot; in 2004. While the project faced setbacks and inactivity, it laid the groundwork for Fandom&#39;s later search engine endeavors. The public alpha of &quot;Wikia Search&quot; was launched in 2008 but was met with mixed reviews and was ultimately discontinued in 2009.</p>
<h2 id="questions-and-answers-site">Questions and Answers Site</h2>
<h3 id="a-quest-for-knowledge">A Quest for Knowledge</h3>
<p>In January 2009, Fandom introduced &quot;Wikianswers,&quot; a question and answer website. This platform allowed users to create topic-specialized knowledge market wikis, expanding the scope of knowledge sharing within the Fandom ecosystem.</p>
<h2 id="controversies">Controversies</h2>
<h3 id="advertising-woes">Advertising Woes</h3>
<p>Fandom communities have occasionally faced challenges related to advertising, including inappropriate or resource-intensive ads. The ownership of domain names by Fandom has also led to difficulties when communities seek new hosting arrangements.</p>
<h3 id="mcdonald-s-grimace-shake-promotion">McDonald&#39;s Grimace Shake Promotion</h3>
<p>In June 2023, Fandom faced scrutiny for replacing content on the McDonald&#39;s Wiki with an advertisement promoting the Grimace Shake. This move sparked controversy, with some community members expressing concerns about the suppression of user-generated content.</p>
<h3 id="minecraft-wiki-departure">Minecraft Wiki Departure</h3>
<p>In July 2023, the Minecraft wiki on Fandom decided to move to another platform, citing reasons such as ad-cluttered experiences and a focus on search engine optimization over the wiki&#39;s interests. This departure highlighted the challenges faced by Fandom in maintaining its community trust.</p>
<h2 id="relationship-with-wikipedia">Relationship with Wikipedia</h2>
<h3 id="a-complex-connection">A Complex Connection</h3>
<p>Throughout the 2000s, Fandom (then known as Wikia) faced accusations of profiting from its perceived association with Wikipedia. While some referred to Fandom as the commercial counterpart to Wikipedia, Wikimedia and Fandom staff disputed this characterization.</p>
<p>In the past, Fandom and Wikimedia Foundation collaborated on various initiatives, sharing hosting and office space. However, they emphasized fairness and neutrality in their arrangements.</p>
